What is Rowing

Rowing has been an Olympic Sport since 1900 and a collegiate sport since the early 19th century.

The sport has gained popularity among youths in recent years because

it is non-contact and the vast scholarship opportunities that followed

Title XI. A shell (technical term for the boat) is composed

of between one and eight rowers. The athlete propels the

shell through the water on 2000 meter (sprint race) and

5000 meter race (head race) courses. The athlete uses their

energy, weight and major muscle groups to pull the oar

through the water (stroke), which in return propels the boat.

Technique plays an important role in reducing drag and maximizing biomechanical efficiency.

The mixture of technique, power and teamwork creates a balanced

sport that can fit into what anyone is looking for in an activity.
